About me

Hello, I am Huangchenran, a junior student at the School of Software Engineering at Tongji University, majoring in Software Engineering.

I intern as a research assistant in Tongji Network and Machine Intelligence Laboratory. I conduct research in the field of edge computing and task offloading and have submitted two papers as the first and second author, respectively. Also, I served as a research assistant at Tongji University's Xlab, where I focused on portrait and audio recognition. Research Experience

Blockchain-Enabled Collaborative Task Offloading for Zero-Trust Vehicular Fog Computing

Tongji Network and Machine Intelligence Laboratory, first author, submitted to IEEE GLOBECOM

  • Proposed a blockchain-enabled zero-trust vehicular fog computing framework.
  • Proposed a multi-attribute offloading and group-based continuous verification scheme.
  • Experimental simulations show that our scheme reduces latency by 18% and increases throughput by 34%.

A Light-Weight and Modular Simulator for UAV Integrated Vehicular Fog Computing

Tongji Network and Machine Intelligence Laboratory, second author, submitted to IEEE TMC (CCF-A)

  • Developed an air-ground collaborative vehicle fog computing (VFC) simulation platform.
  • Introduced a detailed use case of the platform.
  • The simulation results verify the platform's validity and demonstrate its capability to accurately model and analyze complex interactions in UAV-integrated VFC scenarios.

Digital Identity Verification System Based on Multimodality

Tongji xlab laboratory

  • Integrated video and audio modalities for digital identity verification. Improved the ECAPATDNN model and extracted similarities in the audio.
  • Recognized and extracted faces based on the MTCNN network and extracted similarity vectors about faces based on the Inception-Resnet model.
  • Used CLLR to process audio and video similarity vectors and fuse two modal information respectively.
